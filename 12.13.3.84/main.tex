12.13.3.84.Two cards are drawn from a well shuffled deck of 52 playing cards with
replacement. The probability, that both cards are queens, is
\begin{enumerate}[label =\Alph*]
\item $\frac{1}{13} \times \frac{1}{13}$  \item $\frac{1}{13} + \frac{1}{13}$  \item $\frac{1}{13} \times \frac{1}{17}$  \item $\frac{1}{13} \times \frac{4}{51}$
\end{enumerate}
\solution:
Let Q be the event of selecting queens from the deck of 52 cards.There are 4 queens in a standard deck.So,
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
\pr{Q}&= \frac{4}{52}\\
&= \frac{1}{13}
\end{split}
\end{equation}
Let $E_1$ and $E_2$ be the events representing success on first and second draw respectively.Here $E_1$ and $E_2$ are independent.
\begin{equation}
\pr{E_1}= \pr{Q}= \frac{1}{13}
\end{equation}
$\because$ we are drawing with replacement, the probability remains the same as the first draw.So,
\begin{equation}
\pr{E_2}= \pr{Q}= \frac{1}{13}
\end{equation}
Let $S$ be the event that both cards are queens.
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
\pr{S}&= \pr{E_1E_2}\\
&=\pr{E_1} \times \pr{E_2}\\
\therefore \pr{S}&=\frac{1}{13} \times \frac{1}{13}
\end{split}
\end{equation}
